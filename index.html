How pension tax relief works
1
You pay in the net amount. Your provider claims the 20% basic rate top-up directly from HMRC and adds it to your pot.
2
Every £800 you pay becomes £1,000 in your pension automatically — before any higher-rate claim.
3
If you pay 40% or 45% tax, you can claim the extra 20% or 25% back — typically through self-assessment.
4
Between £100k–£125,140, pension contributions also restore your personal allowance — saving an extra 20p in the pound.
Estimates for 2025/26. Tax relief is available on personal contributions up to 100% of UK earnings (minimum £3,600). Standard annual allowance £60,000; MPAA £10,000. Tapered allowance applies where threshold income exceeds £200,000 and adjusted income exceeds £260,000. You must pay sufficient tax at the higher/additional rate to claim that relief. Scottish taxpayers claim additional relief above basic rate (19%) via self-assessment. This is a guide only — verify with HMRC or a financial adviser.
